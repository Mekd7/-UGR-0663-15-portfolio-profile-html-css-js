Tell Me Your Dreams is one of my favourite novels from Sidney Sheldon’s collection. The story follows a young woman grappling with Multiple Personality Disorder, drawing readers into her complex world as she becomes embroiled in a chilling crime scene. Sheldon masterfully sheds light on the challenges of mental health, raising awareness about the disorder in an engaging way. It’s an astonishing piece that combines suspense and depth, making it a compelling read!

As an individual who struggles with decision-making and often contemplates what lies on the other side of choices, I found this book to be almost liberating. The protagonist is caught between two significant life decisions and ultimately chooses the path that costs her one of the greatest opportunities of her life. Living with her regrets, she gains a glimpse into what her alternate decision could have entailed by slipping into a parallel universe. The narrative beautifully explores the themes of fate and the idea that our choices, rather than being mistakes, offer us a different set of experiences.

This book resonates with the saying, "Our childhood can lead us to walk down paths blind to red flags, as we seek the familiarity of what we grew up with and label it as love." I’ll leave it at that! Enjoy!
